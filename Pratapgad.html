About

Pratapgad Fort is a historic fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. It is situated at an elevation of 1,080 meters above sea level and is known for its strategic location and architectural grandeur.

The fort was built in the 17th century by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. It served as a key military stronghold and played a significant role in defending the Maratha kingdom against various invasions.

Pratapgad Fort is famous for the Battle of Pratapgad fought between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan, a prominent general of the Adil Shahi dynasty. This historic battle resulted in a decisive victory for the Marathas and established their dominance in the region.

The fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes, with lush green hills and valleys. It features several notable structures, including the Bhavani Temple, the Afzal Tower, and the Mahadarwaja (main entrance).

Today, Pratapgad Fort attracts tourists and history enthusiasts who are interested in exploring its rich heritage and learning about the valiant tales of the Maratha warriors. The fort's serene surroundings and historical significance make it a popular destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike.
