About

Rajmachi Fort, also known as Udhewadi Fort, is a historical hill fort located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India. It is situated near the towns of Lonavala and Khandala, approximately 15 kilometers from each, and is easily accessible from both Mumbai and Pune. Rajmachi Fort is renowned for its picturesque location, ancient ruins, and stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

History:
Rajmachi Fort has a long history that dates back to the time of the Satavahanas. It later came under the control of various dynasties, including the Yadavas, the Bahamanis, the Marathas, and the British.

The fort was strategically important during various historical periods due to its location on the ancient trade route between Konkan and the Deccan. It served as a military outpost and a strategic point to control the region.

Rajmachi Fort is renowned for its natural beauty and historical significance. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Shrivardhan Fort and Manaranjan Fort: Rajmachi Fort is a twin fort, consisting of two separate hill forts - Shrivardhan and Manaranjan. Both forts are connected by a plateau and can be explored together during the trek.
Uday Sagar Lake: Atop the fort, there is a beautiful lake known as Uday Sagar Lake or Shirota Lake, which serves as a water source.
Ancient Caves: Rajmachi Fort has several ancient caves, which are believed to have been used by monks and ascetics in the past.

Trekking and Tourism:
Rajmachi Fort is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers. The trek to Rajmachi Fort starts from the base village of Udhewadi and takes approximately 2 to 3 hours to reach the summit. The trek offers breathtaking views of the lush green valleys, waterfalls (especially during the monsoon), and the scenic beauty of the Western Ghats.
There are two main trekking routes to reach Rajmachi Fort - one from Lonavala and the other from Karjat. The Lonavala route is shorter and more popular among trekkers.
The best time to visit Rajmachi Fort is during the post-monsoon and winter seasons when the surroundings are green, and the weather is pleasant for trekking. The monsoon season, though enchanting with its lush greenery and waterfalls, can make the trek challenging due to slippery paths.
Today, Rajmachi Fort is a popular weekend getaway and is managed by local authorities. It attracts a significant number of tourists and trekkers who come to explore its historical significance, enjoy the trekking experience, and relish the natural beauty of the Sahyadris.
