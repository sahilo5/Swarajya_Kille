About

Malhargad Fort, also known as Sonori Fort, is a hill fort located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. It is situated near the village of Sonori, approximately 30 kilometers southwest of Pune city. The fort is perched at an elevation of about 3,700 feet (1,128 meters) above sea level, providing stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

History:
TThe history of Malhargad Fort dates back to the 17th century during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. The fort was strategically important as it provided a vantage point to observe the nearby forts of Rajgad and Torna, which were under Shivaji's control.

Malhargad Fort, along with Rajgad and Torna, formed a part of the fortification line known as the "Bhorpya Maval" to safeguard the Maratha kingdom from potential invaders.

Malhargad Fort is a relatively smaller fort compared to some of the more famous ones in the region, but it has its own historical significance. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Entrance Gate: The fort has a simple yet sturdy entrance gate, which is still intact.
Temple: At the top of the fort, there is a small temple dedicated to Lord Khandoba, also known as Malhari Martand, from whom the fort gets its alternative name "Malhargad."
Water Cisterns: Like many forts in the region, Malhargad has rock-cut water cisterns that provided a crucial water source for the fort's occupants.

Trekking and Tourism:
Malhargad Fort is a popular destination for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. The trek to the fort starts from the base village of Sonori and takes around 2 to 3 hours to reach the summit. The trail passes through dense forests and rocky terrain, providing a moderately challenging yet enjoyable trekking experience.
The best time to visit Malhargad Fort is during the monsoon and winter seasons when the surroundings are lush green, and the weather is pleasant for trekking.
As with many historical forts in Maharashtra, Malhargad Fort is now managed and maintained by local authorities and attracts tourists and trekkers who seek to explore its historical significance, enjoy the natural beauty of the Sahyadri mountains, and experience a bit of the region's rich cultural heritage.
