About

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a historically significant hill fort located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India. It is situated near the town of Velhe, approximately 50 kilometers southwest of Pune city. Torna Fort is one of the oldest forts in the region and holds great importance in the history of the Maratha Empire.

History:
Torna Fort has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was originally built by the Shaiva Panth, a religious sect, and was known as Sudhagad. Later, the fort came under the control of various dynasties, including the Bahamanis and the Nizamshahis.

In 1646, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire, captured Torna Fort from the Bijapur Sultanate, marking it as one of the first forts he acquired during his military campaigns. Shivaji renamed the fort as "Prachandagad" (meaning "Massive Fort") due to its impressive size and strategic significance.

Torna Fort is renowned for its historical significance, strong fortifications, and breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Bini Darwaja: The main entrance gate of the fort, which showcases the impressive military architecture of the past.
Kothi Darwaja: Another significant gate on the fort with unique architectural features.
Budhla Machi: A cliff on the fort known for its peculiar shape, resembling the hump of a camel.
Mengai Temple: A small temple dedicated to the goddess Tornaji, situated near the Bini Darwaja.

Trekking and Tourism:
Torna Fort is a popular destination for trekkers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers. The trek to the fort starts from the village of Velhe and takes around 3 to 4 hours to reach the summit. The trek offers a mix of moderate and challenging sections, making it suitable for experienced trekkers.
The best time to visit Torna Fort is during the monsoon and winter seasons when the surroundings are lush green, and the weather is pleasant for trekking. The monsoon season, in particular, enhances the beauty of the region with waterfalls and misty surroundings.
Today, Torna Fort is managed and maintained by local authorities, and it attracts a significant number of visitors who come to explore its historical significance, enjoy the trekking experience, and immerse themselves in the rich history of the Maratha Empire.
