About

Sajjangad Fort is a historic fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. It was built in the 17th century by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort served as a prominent stronghold and played a significant role in the Maratha Empire.

Sajjangad Fort is known for its strategic location and served as a refuge for Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj during his lifetime. The fort is situated on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

One of the notable features of Sajjangad Fort is the Samadhi (tomb) of Sant Ramdas, a renowned saint and spiritual advisor to Shivaji Maharaj. The Samadhi attracts devotees and pilgrims from far and wide.

The fort holds historical significance and stands as a testament to the bravery and valor of the Maratha warriors. Today, it serves as a popular tourist destination and offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history and architectural grandeur of the Maratha era.

The fort can be reached by trekking up the hill, and visitors can explore various structures within its premises, including the temple, prayer halls, and other architectural remnants. The serene surroundings, coupled with the historical importance of Sajjangad Fort, make it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers.
