About

Vasota Fort, also known as Vyaghragad, is a historic hill fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. It is situated within the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary, surrounded by the lush greenery of the Sahyadri mountain range. Vasota Fort is known for its scenic beauty, rich biodiversity, and challenging trekking routes.

History:
Vasota Fort has a long history that dates back to ancient times. It was originally built by the Shilahara dynasty and later came under the control of various rulers, including the Marathas and the British.

The fort gained prominence during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj when it became part of the Swarajya (self-rule) movement against various invaders. However, the fort was eventually captured by the British in the early 19th century.

Vasota Fort is renowned for its picturesque location and the surrounding natural beauty. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Forest Trails: The trek to Vasota Fort starts from the village of Bamnoli and involves a scenic trek through dense forests, with opportunities to spot various flora and fauna.
Koyana River: The trek also includes a boat ride across the beautiful Koyana River to reach the base of the fort.
Nageshwar Temple: At the top of the fort, there is a small temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as the Nageshwar Temple.
Vasota Wildlife Sanctuary: The fort is located within the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to diverse wildlife, including tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and various bird species.

Trekking and Tourism:
Vasota Fort is a popular destination for trekkers and nature lovers. The trek to the fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, including the lush forests, waterfalls, and the vast Koyna backwaters.
The trek to Vasota Fort is moderately challenging and requires a certain level of physical fitness. It takes approximately 3 to 4 hours to reach the summit. The best time to visit Vasota Fort is during the post-monsoon and winter seasons when the weather is pleasant, and the surroundings are lush green.
Visitors to Vasota Fort can enjoy the serene environment, experience the thrill of trekking through the wilderness, and immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the Sahyadris.
Please note that as Vasota Fort is located within the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary, visitors are required to obtain necessary permits from the forest authorities before undertaking the trek. The fort is also a protected area, and visitors are advised to follow responsible trekking practices to preserve its ecological balance and natural beauty.
