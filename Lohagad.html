About

Lohagad Fort, also known as Iron Fort, is a historic hill fort located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. It is situated near the town of Lonavala in the Pune district, approximately 65 kilometers from Pune city and about 95 kilometers from Mumbai. The fort stands at an elevation of around 3,450 feet (1,052 meters) above sea level.

History:
Lohagad Fort has a long and illustrious history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed to have been originally built during the Satavahana period, making it over 2,000 years old. The fort changed hands multiple times and was under the control of various dynasties, including the Satavahanas, Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, and Yadavas.

During the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, Lohagad Fort was captured from the Mughals in 1648 CE. Shivaji Maharaj used the fort strategically to store his treasury and strengthen his hold in the region. The fort was later taken over by the British in the early 19th century.

Lohagad Fort is renowned for its historical significance, impressive architecture, and stunning views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the surrounding lush green landscape. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Entrance Gates: Lohagad has several gates, including the Ganesh Darwaja, Narayan Darwaja, and Hanuman Darwaja, each with unique architectural features.
Fortifications: The fort is well-fortified with strong walls and bastions, some of which are still in good condition.
Vinchu Kata: Vinchu Kata is a unique feature of the fort that resembles the shape of a scorpion's tail. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys.
Pavana Lake: The Pavana Lake lies at the base of the fort, adding to the scenic beauty of the area.

Trekking and Tourism:
Lohagad Fort is a popular destination for trekkers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers. The trek to the fort starts from the village of Lohagadwadi and takes around 1.5 to 2 hours to reach the summit. The trek is relatively easy, making it suitable for beginners and families.
The best time to visit Lohagad Fort is during the monsoon and winter seasons when the surroundings are lush green and the weather is pleasant for trekking. The monsoon season is particularly captivating as the fort and its surroundings get enveloped in a misty and magical atmosphere.
Today, Lohagad Fort is managed and maintained by local authorities, and it attracts a significant number of tourists who come to explore its historical significance, enjoy the trekking experience, and relish the natural beauty of the Western Ghats.
