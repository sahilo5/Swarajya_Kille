About

Vijaydurg Fort, also known as Victory Fort, is a historic coastal fort located in the Sindhudurg district of Maharashtra, India. It is situated on the western coast of Maharashtra, along the Arabian Sea, near the town of Devgad. Vijaydurg Fort is one of the oldest and most well-preserved sea forts in Maharashtra and holds significant historical importance.

History:
Vijaydurg Fort has a long and eventful history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed to have been originally built during the reign of the Shilahara dynasty around the 12th century. Later, the fort came under the control of various dynasties, including the Bahamanis, the Adilshahis, and the Marathas.

Vijaydurg Fort has a long and eventful history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed to have been originally built during the reign of the Shilahara dynasty around the 12th century. Later, the fort came under the control of various dynasties, including the Bahamanis, the Adilshahis, and the Marathas.

The fort gained prominence during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj when it came under Maratha control. Shivaji captured the fort in 1653 CE and renamed it "Vijaydurg" to commemorate his victory over the fort's previous rulers. Under the Marathas, the fort underwent extensive renovations and was further strengthened to enhance its military capabilities.
Vijaydurg Fort is renowned for its impressive architecture, strong fortifications, and maritime history. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Entrance Gates: The fort has multiple entrance gates, each with unique architectural features, including the Gopinath and Hanuman gates.
Citadel: The central part of the fort, known as the "Citadel," is a strategically important area with bastions and watchtowers.
Secret Tunnel: Vijaydurg Fort is said to have a secret underground tunnel that leads to the nearby cliffs and served as an escape route during sieges.
Durga Devi Temple: There is a small temple dedicated to Goddess Durga inside the fort complex.

Trekking and Tourism:
Vijaydurg Fort is a popular tourist destination, attracting history enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers. The fort is accessible by road and can be reached from Devgad town.
Visitors to the fort can explore the historic ruins, enjoy the panoramic views of the Arabian Sea, and learn about the maritime history associated with the fort. The surrounding natural beauty and the fort's historical significance make it an appealing destination for tourists looking to delve into Maharashtra's rich cultural heritage.
Vijaydurg Fort is now managed and maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) and continues to be a site of historical and architectural importance in Maharashtra.
