About

Panhala Fort is a historic hill fort located in the Kolhapur district of Maharashtra, India. It is one of the largest and most significant forts in the state, situated approximately 20 kilometers northwest of the city of Kolhapur. The fort is perched at an elevation of around 3,937 feet (1,200 meters) above sea level in the Sahyadri mountain range.

History:
Panhala Fort has a long and illustrious history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed to have been built during the 12th century, making it over 800 years old. The fort has seen several rulers and dynasties, including the Shilahara dynasty, the Bahamanis, the Adilshahis, the Marathas, and the British.

The fort gained significant importance during the reign of the Maratha Empire and became one of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's important forts. Shivaji captured the fort in 1659 CE after a strategic military operation and maintained control over it until his death in 1680. Panhala Fort played a crucial role in Shivaji's defense strategy and as a safe location for his family during times of conflict.

Panhala Fort is renowned for its historical significance, impressive architecture, and scenic beauty. Some of the key features of the fort include:

Teen Darwaza: The main entrance gate to the fort, which is an impressive structure with intricate carvings.
Andhar Bavai: A hidden passage inside the fort, which was used as an escape route during times of danger.
Kalavanticha Mahal: A palace built on the fort, believed to be the residence of the Maratha queen Rani Tarabai.
Ambarkhana: A granary constructed to store food supplies during the fort's active period.
Sajja Kothi: A pavilion where Shivaji would hold meetings and address his soldiers.

Trekking and Tourism:
Panhala Fort is a popular destination for tourists, trekkers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers. The trek to the fort starts from the base of Panhala village and takes around 30 minutes to reach the summit. The trek is relatively easy, making it suitable for people of all age groups.
The fort offers panoramic views of the picturesque landscape, including the Sahyadri mountains, the lush green valleys, and the nearby Pavankhind gorge, a site of historical significance.
Panhala Fort is now managed and maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) and attracts a significant number of visitors who come to explore its historical significance, enjoy the trekking experience, and immerse themselves in the rich history of the Maratha Empire and the region.
